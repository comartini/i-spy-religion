About This Project

Where is Religion? is an interactive teaching and learning tool aimed at exploring the unexpected places where religion appears in American life. Sometimes it is obvious, sometimes it is hidden, sometimes it appears in the guise of the secular or emerges as a material mixture of several traditions. Click around and find out!

How to Use This Site

The site works by randomly displaying a Scene, which will change if you refresh the page or click Load a New Scene. When a Scene appears, click around in the image to try and find the Hotspots, or places where religion appears. If you find a Hotspot, a text box will appear that explains the Hotspot's content. The total number of Hotspots for each Scene is displayed below the image. All of the Scenes, unless otherwise noted, are Public Domain images from Wikimedia or the Library of Congress.

Further Reading

This project was inspired in large part by Sally Prommey's Religion in Plain View: Public Aesthetics of American Display (University of Chicago Press, 2024) and Matthew Engelke's essay Angels in Swindon (American Ethnologist, 2012). Further readings are linked in many of the Hotspot texts.
